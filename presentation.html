Enrichment APIs

5 public APIs, fired in parallel

🏛️
US Census + ACS
No key required

Population, renter %, median rent, median HH income. Core multifamily fit signals.

📰
NewsAPI
Free tier

3 most recent headlines about the company. Signals growth, funding, or distress.

📖
Wikipedia REST
No key required

Company page existence + summary. Signals legitimacy and portfolio scale.

🌤️
OpenWeather
Free tier

Current conditions at city. Used as an email icebreaker — not scored.

🏦
FRED (Federal Reserve)
Free tier

National rental vacancy rate. Low vacancy = high demand = better fit for EliseAI.

Fail-soft design

If any API is down or rate-limited, pipeline continues. Affected fields return null. Score uses remaining signals.

Responses cached 24h — demo reruns are instant.

Lead Scoring

7 signals, 0–100 score

Renter occupied share 25 pts
City / metro population 20 pts
Median rent 14 pts
Wikipedia presence 13 pts
Recent news (≤90 days) 12 pts
Median HH income 8 pts
FRED rental vacancy rate 8 pts
🔥 Hot ≥ 75

Large renter-heavy metro, known company, active in news. Prioritize immediately.

⚡ Warm 50–74

Decent market, some signals present. Worth a personalized touch.

❄️ Cold < 50

Small market, low renter share, or unknown company. Lower priority.

Why these signals? EliseAI sells AI leasing assistants to multifamily operators. High renter share + big metro = more units = more ROI from automation. Wikipedia/news = company is real and active.

Assumptions & Scoring Logic

Documented reasoning

Scoring assumptions
1
Renter share is the top signal (25 pts) — EliseAI only helps with rental units. Low renter share means small addressable portfolio.
2
Population proxies portfolio size (20 pts) — Large metros have more units, more leasing volume, more ROI from AI.
3
Wikipedia presence signals legitimacy (13 pts) — Operators with Wikipedia pages manage at scale. Small landlords are unlikely buyers.
4
News recency signals active company (12 pts) — Companies in the news are growing or moving. Stale companies may not be investing in tech.
ICP assumptions
  • Target: multifamily operators managing ≥100 units in a single market
  • Best fit: NMHC Top 50 operators or regional players in high-renter cities
  • Renter % >50% + population >500K = core sweet spot
  • Weights are tunable — historical replay in Week 1 will calibrate
  • Weather API is an icebreaker only, never affects score
  • Country field accepted but not scored — US Census handles domestic only
If weights are wrong
Week 1 historical replay against CRM outcomes will show tier vs. SQL correlation. Retune before any SDR touches it.
Project Plan

6-week rollout

Week 1
MVP validation (internal) — Run on 50 historic CRM leads. Compare tier vs. actual SQL outcome. Calibration report. Retune weights if needed before any SDR sees it.
Week 2
Closed pilot — 2 friendly SDRs — Live inbound leads, parallel to normal process. Daily 15-min sync. Tune weights + email template daily. Exit: both SDRs save ≥10 min/lead.
Weeks 3–4
Team pilot — one SDR pod (~5 reps) — 30-min training + runbook. CRM webhook integration (RevOps). Adoption logging. Weekly retro. Exit: >70% adoption, no data issues.
Weeks 5–6
GA rollout — all SDRs — Recorded training video. Overnight cron: every new CRM lead pre-enriched by 9 AM. Public dashboard: adoption + score→SQL conversion + minutes saved.
Post-GA
Next quarter — Salesforce Lightning card if adoption holds. Paid data layer (Clearbit/Apollo) if free API accuracy is insufficient at scale.
